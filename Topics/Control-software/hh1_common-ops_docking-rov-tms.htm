Docking the ROV to the TMS

Docking the ROV to the TMS requires simultaneously managing ROV motion and paying in the tether.

WARNING!  Do not lift the TMS and ROV unless they are fully docked.

Docking for the HD and UHD-III Systems

1. In the TMS CONTROL panel of the FLIGHT screen, confirm that the latches are closed.
2. Fly the ROV to the TMS while paying in tether. Keep some slack in the tether.

Note:  If you are looking at bullet camera output while you are flying the ROV backward to the TMS, you may want to fly in REVERSE mode (see REVERSE Auto Function). Using REVERSE mode may make flight more intuitive.

3. Fly the ROV to a position below and offset from the TMS so that you can clearly see the base of the TMS and the tether. Leave some slack in the tether and stop paying in. Monitor TMS movement to determine the heave.
4. Correct any tether turns as required.
5. When heave is at a minimum, and while keeping the base of the TMS and tether/bullet in view, pay in tether and fly in under the TMS.
6. Center the bullet under the docking cone as the bullet enters and latches close behind the bullet.
7. If possible, visually confirm that the bullet is fully caged (the top of the bullet is visible above the latches).
8. In the TMS CONTROL panel of the FLIGHT screen, confirm that the latches are fully closed.

The ROV and TMS are now safe to lift.

WARNING!  DO NOT LIFT the TMS and ROV unless all latch and bullet symbols on the TMS CONTROL are green. If not all symbols are green, the ROV and TMS are NOT fully docked and are NOT safe to lift.

Docking for the UHD-II

In its basic form, docking the ROV with the TMS consists of:

● Paying in tether until the bullet is inside the secondary latches.
● Latching the primary latches.

To dock the ROV to the TMS:

1. Ensure that you are not paying out tether. (This ensures that the secondary latches will be closed.)
2. In the TMS CONTROL panel of the FLIGHT screen, confirm that the secondary latches are closed and the primary latches are open.
3. Fly the ROV to the TMS while paying in tether. Keep some slack in the tether.

Note:  If you are looking at bullet camera output while you are flying the ROV backward to the TMS, you may want to fly in REVERSE mode (see REVERSE Auto Function). Using REVERSE mode may make flight more intuitive.

4. Fly the ROV to a position below and offset from the TMS so that you can clearly see the base of the TMS and the tether. Leave some slack in the tether and stop paying in. Monitor TMS movement to determine the heave.
5. Correct any tether turns as required.
6. When heave is at a minimum, and while keeping the base of the TMS and tether/bullet in view, pay in tether and fly in under the TMS.
7. Center the bullet under the docking area.
8. Pay in tether until the bullet reaches the secondary latches. The secondary latches automatically open to let in the bullet and then automatically close behind it.

9. Stop paying in tether.
10. Once the bullet is inside the secondary latches, the LATCH button in the TMS CONTROL panel becomes available for closing the primary latches. Tap the LATCH button, and the primary latches lift the bullet into the final docking position and then latch behind the bullet.
11. In the TMS CONTROL panel of the FLIGHT screen, confirm that all latch and bullet symbols are green.

12. Using the TMS docking camera, visually confirm that the bullet is fully caged (the top of the bullet is visible above the latches).
13. In the TMS TETHER CONTROL panel (one of the TMS diagnostics screens), view the LATCH CONTROL section. The bullet position indicator should read PRIMARIES.

If you have both visual and screen confirmation that the bullet is fully latched by the primary latches, the ROV and TMS are safe to lift.

WARNING!  DO NOT LIFT the TMS and ROV unless all latch and bullet symbols on the TMS CONTROL are green. If not all symbols are green, the ROV and TMS are NOT fully docked and are NOT safe to lift.
