Undocking for the HD and UHD-III Systems

WARNING!  Using the UNLATCH function when TMS MASTER OVERRIDE is in force can cause the ROV to unexpectedly separate from the TMS, potentially resulting in death, injury, and/or equipment loss. Use good judgment and follow all appropriate safety policies when you operate TMS latches in MASTER OVERRIDE.

1. Display the FLIGHT screen.
2. Ensure that TMS control is active (as indicated by the TMS CONTROL button).
3. Issue a command to pay out tether, and the UNLATCH ALL button on the TMS CONTROL panel becomes available.
4. To open the latches, tap the UNLATCH button and drag the arrow to the unlock icon. The command to open the primary and secondary latches will remain in effect for 15 seconds.

Note:   The TMS will not actively pay out tether until the ROV has pulled approximately 0.5 m of tether off the drum.

Note:  The secondary latches open 0.25 seconds after the primary latches, ensuring that the primary latches open first.

5. Apply upward thrust to the ROV to unload the weight of the ROV from the primary latches.
6. Maintain upward thrust until both primary and secondary latches are open. Then apply downward thrust to move the ROV bullet clear of the primary and secondary latches.

Note:  The latches will not open until the weight is taken off them, either by the ROV thrusting up or because of heave.

Note:  The latches will only open when commanded by the UNLATCH button in the FLIGHT screen TMS CONTROL panel. Paying tether out or in does not affect the latch state.

7. Once approximately 0.5 m of tether has been rendered, the TMS will become able to actively pay out tether. Fly the ROV straight down from the TMS while you pay out tether.
8. In the TMS CONTROL panel, verify that the latches have closed.

Latch States After Undocking

After the ROV and TMS are undocked, the TMS latches automatically close. You do not need open the latches later for docking because the latches automatically open as the ROV bullet is drawn into the bullet cage.

Undocking for the UHD-II System

WARNING!  Using the UNLATCH function when TMS MASTER OVERRIDE is in force can cause the ROV to unexpectedly separate from the TMS, potentially resulting in death, injury, and/or equipment loss. Use good judgment and follow all appropriate safety policies when you operate TMS latches in MASTER OVERRIDE.

When you undock the ROV from the TMS, you do not need to pay in tether to unload the primary latches before you open the latches. The primary latches are designed to safely support the ROV load for final release of the ROV bullet.

When you issue a command to pay out tether, the secondary latches automatically open. However, no tether is typically paid out until the primary latches are fully open (because the control system disables TMS drum and pinch wheel motors until the primary latches are open). However, this TMS motor interlock does not apply if TMS MASTER OVERRIDE is in force.

In its basic form, undocking the ROV and the TMS consists of:

● Issuing a command to pay out tether (automatically opening the secondary latches).
● Thrusting down.
● Opening the primary latches to release the bullet.

To undock the ROV from the TMS:

1. Display the FLIGHT screen.
2. Ensure that TMS control is active (as indicated by the TMS CONTROL button).
3. Ensure that the ROV is submerged at least 5 meters. (Required before the TMS latches can open, unless TMS MASTER OVERRIDE is in force.)
4. On the FLIGHT screen TMS CONTROL panel, verify that the ROV and TMS are completely docked.
5. Command tether payout. This causes the secondary latches to automatically open, but the TMS motor interlock prevents tether payout (unless TMS MASTER OVERRIDE is in force).
6. Command ROV downward thrust. The ROV will not move because it is being held by the primary latches, but commanding thrust now will start the ROV moving as soon as the primary latches open.
7. On the TMS CONTROL panel, open the primary latches (tap the UNLATCH button and drag the arrow to the unlock icon). When the latches are fully open, the drum and pinch wheel motors begin to pay out tether. Because you previously commanded downward ROV thrust, the ROV will move away from the TMS.
8. Fly the ROV straight down from the TMS while you pay out tether.

Latch States After Undocking

As long as you continue to command tether payout, the secondary latches remain open. As soon as you stop commanding tether payout (or you command tether pay-in), the secondary latches close.

After the undocking is complete, primary latches remain open until you use the TMS CONTROL panel to close them. However, you can leave the latches open so that they are in the correct state for later docking the ROV and TMS.
