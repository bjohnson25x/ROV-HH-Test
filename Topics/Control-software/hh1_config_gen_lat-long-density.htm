Setting Latitude, Longitude, and Water Density

You must enter accurate latitude and water density information to account for changes in water pressure. Inaccurate latitude and water density information can cause inaccurate depth readings.

If the ROV system is equipped for Mid-Water StationKeep, you must also use this panel to enter longitude. Accurate latitude and longitude references are important for Mid-Water StationKeep operation.

Note:  Enter the latitude, longitude, and water density while the ROV is on deck before a dive.

1. Ensure that the user profile for your control station includes supervisor permissions.
2. On any CONFIGURATION screen, tap the GENERAL tab.
3. On the list on the left side of the screen, tap ENVIRONMENT.
4. Tap the button for the item that you want to change.
5. Enter numbers into the screen keypad.

For water density, allowable limits are 0.89 to 1.1 grams per cubic centimeter.

● Standard fresh water density is approximately 1.0 grams per cubic centimeter.
● Standard seawater density is approximately 1.028 grams per cubic centimeter.

Note:  For the CDL TOGS NAV navigation instrument, you cannot set latitude and longitude in the ROV control system software. Instead, you must use configuration software provided by the instrument manufacturer.
