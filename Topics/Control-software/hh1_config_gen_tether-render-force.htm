Adjusting Tether Render Force

See Also for links to information about configuring TMS tether.

Note:  Information in this section applies only to TMS units that use DC motors rather than VFD motors.

Rendering occurs when tether is being pulled off the drum by some force other than the TMS motors (for example, by ROV movement or water currents). The control system always applies some force from TMS motors to resist rendering. However, in some conditions (for example, when strong currents are present), you may want to increase the resistance to rendering.

To increase the resistance to rendering:

1. Ensure that the user profile for your control station includes supervisor permissions.
2. On any CONFIGURATION screen, tap the GENERAL tab.
3. On the list on the left side of the screen, tap TMS.
4. In the STATIC RENDER FORCE panel, tap the UP or DOWN button to increase or decrease the render forcee.

Note:  For any given render force value, the control system automatically increases the force as more tether is paid out, and decreases the force as tether is paid in. For example, if you have a render force value of 15%, the control system will apply more resistance when the drum is nearly empty than when it is nearly full.
