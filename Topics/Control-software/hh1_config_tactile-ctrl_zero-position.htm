Setting Control Device Neutral (Zero) Position

See Also for links to general information about configuring tactile controls.

Mechanical wear or drift in a flight joystick or foot pedal can cause the device to unexpectedly operate even though it is at its mechanical neutral position (not being moved). To correct this condition, you can “zero” flight joysticks and foot pedals.

1. Display any CONFIGURATION screen.
2. Tap the TACTILE CONTROLS tab, and then tap the JOYSTICK CONTROL tab.
3. Use the top-right button to select the desired control station (LEFT CLIENT COMPUTER or RIGHT CLIENT COMPUTER).
4. Inspect all flight joysticks and foot pedals for foreign objects, contamination, damage, and/or wear that could prevent the devices from returning to or holding at the neutral position. Correct as needed.
5. Confirm that all joysticks and foot pedals at their normal mechanical neutral positions.

Caution!   If a joystick or foot pedal is away from its mechanical neutral (zero) position when you tap the ZERO POD CONTROLS button, the device will suddenly activate when the joystick or pedal returns to its neutral position.

6. Tap ZERO POD CONTROLS.

7. Select the other control station and repeat the procedure.
