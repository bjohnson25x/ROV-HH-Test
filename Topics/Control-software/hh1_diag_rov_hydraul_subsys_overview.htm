About the ROV Hydraulics Subsystem Diagnostics Display

See Also for links to diagnostics information for the ROV hydraulic subsystem.

To view status and diagnostics information for the ROV hydraulics subsystem, navigate to the subsystem screen display.

See how

From the Main Menu:

1. Tap DIAGNOSTICS.
2. Tap SYSTEM.
3. When the diagnostics dashboard displays, tap the button for the desired subsystem (like HYDRAULICS, COMPENSATION, or LIGHTS) on the desired platform (like ROV or TMS).

Or from any DIAGNOSTICS screen:

1. Tap the platform drop-down button on the upper left, and then tap the desired platform (like ROV or TMS).
2. Tap a button on the left to select the desired subsystem (like HYDRAULICS, COMPENSATION, or LIGHTS).

Tap HYDRAULICS to display summary information for ROV hydraulic components.

Screen controls are different depending on whether the ROV system has a UHD-III hydraulic configuration or HD/UHD-II hydraulic configuration.

You can briefly run an HPU on deck as part of diagnosing hydraulic problems. If you must run the HPU for more than a brief time, connect a deck cart to the system.

Caution!  Heat generated by extended on-deck operation of ROV or TMS equipment can damage the equipment and related components. During on-deck operations, do not override temperature safety limits set in the ROV control system software. Overriding safety limits can result in component damage or failure. When equipment is running in air, do not touch the equipment to test temperature.

Feedback from HPU Calibration

The DIAGNOSTICS summary screen for hydraulics (ROV hydraulics, or for a hydraulic TMS, TMS hydraulics) contains information concerning the latest calibration for the HPU circuits.

The commanded pressure and pressure feedback from the pressure sensor should track each other closely. If they do not, the HPU circuit may need to be calibrated.
