To view status and diagnostics information for the TMS power subsystem, navigate to the subsystem screen display.

See how

From the Main Menu:

1. Tap DIAGNOSTICS.
2. Tap SYSTEM.
3. When the diagnostics dashboard displays, tap the button for the desired subsystem (like HYDRAULICS, COMPENSATION, or LIGHTS) on the desired platform (like ROV or TMS).

Or from any DIAGNOSTICS screen:

1. Tap the platform drop-down button on the upper left, and then tap the desired platform (like ROV or TMS).
2. Tap a button on the left to select the desired subsystem (like HYDRAULICS, COMPENSATION, or LIGHTS).

Tap POWER to display summary information for TMS power devices.

TMS Motor Power Status and Faults (DC Motors Only)

If the TMS uses DC motors (rather than VFD motors), the 340 VDC button indicates the state of the 340 VDC circuit that powers the motors. (This circuit automatically turns on when you enable TMS IPDS power.)

Note:  You cannot manually disable TMS motor power using this button. To manually disable motor power, you must disable TMS control on the FLIGHT screen (see TMS Screen Controls and Displays) or on the tether control diagnostics screen (see About the Tether Control Diagnostics Display).

The control system has two general categories of TMS motor power circuit faults:

● Critical faults: serious faults (like a motor short circuit or electrical failure of the HV Pak on the TMS IPDS); these faults automatically disable power to the TMS motors.
● Noncritical faults: faults that typically occur when a TMS motor is close to exceeding safe operation limits. The control system does not automatically disable power to the TMS motors, but you should fix conditions that might be causing the problem (for example, you could slow down tether pay-in speed).

For both critical and noncritical TMS motor faults, a RESET button appears below the 340 VDC power button.

For a noncritical fault, you can tap the RESET button to clear the fault. If the condition that caused the fault is no longer in effect (for example, if you have slowed tether pay-in speed), the fault will not reoccur.

For a critical fault, you can:

● Tap the 340 VDC button. This action clears the fault and reenables motor power. If the condition that caused the fault is no longer in effect, the fault clears and you can resume motor operation. If the condition that caused the fault is still in effect, the 340 VDC button will again become disabled, and you must diagnose the fault.
● Tap the RESET button. This action clears the fault without reenabling motor power. If the condition that caused the fault is no longer in effect, the fault clears and you can then tap the 340 VDC button to enable motor power. If the condition that caused the fault is still in effect, you must diagnose the fault.
