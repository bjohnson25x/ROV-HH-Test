Performing TMS Override Functions

See Also for links to diagnostics information for other tether control functions.

See Also for links to diagnostics information for other TMS subsystems.

The TMS MASTER OVERRIDE function lets you perform TMS actions that the control system normally does not allow. In unusual conditions, you may need to override normal TMS control restrictions (for example, to pay out tether when the ROV is docked to the TMS) even at the risk of damaging equipment.

WARNING!  Using the UNLATCH function when TMS MASTER OVERRIDE is in force can cause the ROV to unexpectedly separate from the TMS, potentially resulting in death, injury, and/or equipment loss. Use good judgment and follow all appropriate safety policies when you operate TMS latches in MASTER OVERRIDE.

When the TMS control is active and the MASTER OVERRIDE button is active, you can perform the following actions that are normally blocked:

● Pay out tether beyond the set limit. Be aware that fully extending the tether can damage it, especially where it connects to the tether drum.
● Pay out tether when a TMS latch is closed or in the process of closing or opening. You may need to do this if the latch is open far enough to free the ROV bullet but the latch sensor does not report that the latch is open.
● Pay out tether when the ROV is docked to the TMS.
● Use the UNLATCH button, even if it would not normally be available.
● For the UHD-II only, override the depth interlock that prevents the ROV from being unlatched from the TMS until the ROV is submerged at 5 meters or more. This lets you perform TMS operations on deck (including separating the ROV and TMS on deck.)\
● For the UHD-II, pay tether in or out even if the ROV control system has lost communication with the hydraulic manifold that controls TMS motors for the drum and pinch wheel assembly motors.

If equipment problems prevent you from paying in tether and you cannot latch the ROV to the TMS, using the MASTER OVERRIDE function may allow a normal ROV/TMS recovery.

For the UHD-II, if the primary latches are not fully latched or unlatched upon system startup (or if one latch is open and the other is closed), the LATCH/UNLATCH buttons normally are unavailable for latching or unlatching the ROV. If this happens, you can tap the MASTER OVERRIDE button and use the LATCH/UNLATCH buttons to move both primary latches to fully open or fully closed as required to safely latch or unlatch the ROV.

If possible, confirm or diagnose latch operations while the ROV and TMS are separated. If they cannot be separated, and you are performing diagnostics operations on deck, ensure that both units are supported so that they stay together and are stable regardless of latch positions. Use good judgment and observe appropriate safety precautions as you perform diagnostics activities.

Procedure for TMS Override

1. Ensure that the user profile for your control station includes supervisor permissions.
2. Access MASTER OVERRIDE in one of these ways:
a. From the TMS panel of the DIAGNOSTICS screen, tap TETHER CONTROL.
b. From the TMS panel of the FLIGHT screen, long press anywhere in the panel to display the context menu.
3. Tap MASTER OVERRIDE and drag the arrow to the unlock icon to activate the button.
4. Perform the necessary tether operations.
5. When you have finished performing the necessary operations, deactivate MASTER OVERRIDE (by tapping the MASTER OVERRIDE button) to reapply the control system’s TMS restrictions.
6. If appropriate, change to a different user profile so that the operator no longer has supervisor permissions.
