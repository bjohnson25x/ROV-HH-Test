To view status and diagnostics information for the tether control subsystem, navigate to the subsystem screen display.

See how

From the Main Menu:

1. Tap DIAGNOSTICS.
2. Tap SYSTEM.
3. When the diagnostics dashboard displays, tap the button for the desired subsystem (like HYDRAULICS, COMPENSATION, or LIGHTS) on the desired platform (like ROV or TMS).

Or from any DIAGNOSTICS screen:

1. Tap the platform drop-down button on the upper left, and then tap the desired platform (like ROV or TMS).
2. Tap a button on the left to select the desired subsystem (like HYDRAULICS, COMPENSATION, or LIGHTS).

Tap TETHER CONTROL to display summary information for TMS latches and motors.

Note:  You must be operating in MASTER OVERRIDE mode to control tether and latches from this screen. (Joystick and foot pedal controls continue to operate in MASTER OVERRIDE mode.) If you are not operating in MASTER OVERRIDE mode and you want to operate the TMS from the touch screen, use tether controls on the FLIGHT screen.

The tether control subsystem overview shows latch and TMS motor information. If you have enabled the TMS MASTER OVERRIDE function, you can also control tether and latches from this screen. (For normal operation, you should use tether controls on the FLIGHT screen.)

Screen Display for the HD and UHD-III Systems

Example of tether control subsystem data details and controls:

Fault conditions for the TMS drum drive motor and pinch wheel motor cause automatic motor shutdown. Fault conditions can include:

● Over-voltage or under-voltage
● Over-current
● Over-temperature

Example of latch data and controls:

For the speed feedback gauges, zero (no movement) is in the center of the gauge. Movement (indicated by a white field) goes to maximum speed for tether in (on the gauge left side) to maximum speed for tether out (on the gauge right side).

In the LATCH CONTROL screen section, the vertical blue line represents the latch position. If the line is anywhere inside the gray area on the right, the latches are open and the ROV bullet can be undocked from the TMS.

TMS Sensor Assignments on the Position Sensor Array

TMS sensors (for latches, pinch roller, and diamond screw) are attached to a position sensor array (PSA). You must configure the PSA to indicate which TMS sensor is attached to each array position (see Configuring TMS Position Sensor Array (PSA) Properties (Electric TMS Only)).

To view the current position assignment of each sensor, use the diagnostics screen for the TMS PSA. From the diagnostics dashboard, select the TMS tether control subsystem display and then select the TMS position sensor.

Screen Display for the UHD-II System

Example of tether control subsystem data details and controls:

For the speed feedback gauges, zero (no movement) is in the center of the gauge. Movement (indicated by a white field) goes from maximum speed for tether in (on the gauge left side) to maximum speed for tether out (on the gauge right side).

Example of latch data and controls:

In the LATCH CONTROL screen section, the vertical blue line represents the latch position. The position can be anywhere in the range from completely closed (the blue line is at the left end of the bar) to completely open (the blue line is at the right end of the bar).

The LATCH button for the primary latches is available if:

● The TMS CONTROL button is active and the latches are open.

or

● MASTER OVERRIDE is on.

The UNLATCH button for the primary latches is available if:

● The TMS CONTROL button is active, latches are closed, the ROV is submerged to at least 5 meters, and tether is being paid in or paid out.

or

● MASTER OVERRIDE is on.
