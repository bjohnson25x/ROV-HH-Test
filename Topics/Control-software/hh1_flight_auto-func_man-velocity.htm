MANUAL VELOCITY Features
Purpose Allows dynamic braking, which minimizes overshoot due to ROV momentum.
Access On the FLIGHT screen, tap OTHER and then tap MANUAL VELOCITY.
Turning on/off

Tap the desired button for the MANUAL VELOCITY control (LATERAL, VERTICAL, and/or HEADING).
Function interactions Control is based on inputs from the DVL, depth sensor, and gyro/MRU.

Flight control has two modes: normal mode and manual velocity mode.

● Normal velocity. In this mode, thruster rpm is determined by joystick position. When you return the joystick to the neutral position, the thrusters stop. The ROV's momentum typically causes continued movement.
● Manual velocity. In this mode, ROV movement across the seafloor is determined by joystick position. Returning the joystick to the neutral position does not simply stop the thrusters. Instead, it commands zero velocity, causing the control system to apply reverse thrust to stop the ROV. (This feature is called dynamic braking.) In this mode, control is based on inputs from the DVL, depth sensor, and gyro/MRU.

The MANUAL VELOCITY panel lets you turn on manual velocity control separately for:

● LATERAL movement (fore/aft and starboard/port)
● VERTICAL movement (up/down)
● HEADING movement (yaw)

To turn MANUAL VELOCITY on or off, tap LATERAL, VERTICAL, and/or HEADING. A pilot typically turns on all three for manual velocity control, but you can also turn on just one or two of the controls.
