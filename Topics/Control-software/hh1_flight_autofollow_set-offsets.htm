Setting Offsets for AutoFollow Movement

See Also for links to AutoFollow (manipulator tracking) information.

Once you have enabled AutoFollow for a pan and tilt, the pan and tilt automatically moves when the manipulator moves, keeping the manipulator jaws in sight. If you move the pan and tilt with standard controls while AutoFollow is active, you introduce an offset to the pan and tilt movement that remains until you disable AutoFollow. For example, you might want to introduce an offset so that the camera on the pan and tilt points slightly ahead of the manipulator jaws to view a tool held in the jaws.

To introduce an offset:

1. Enable AutoFollow for the desired camera (see Enabling AutoFollow Operation).
2. Using the PAN AND TILT CONTROL in the CAMERAS panel, move the pan and tilt to introduce the desired offset. If you need to adjust the offset, move the pan and tilt again.
