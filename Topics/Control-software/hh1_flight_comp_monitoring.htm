Strategies for Monitoring Compensator/Reservoir Volume

See Also for links to information about compensator/reservoir monitoring.

During descent, monitor compensators and reservoirs and note any changes. You should expect a slight decrease in volume due to trapped air when the ROV system is still near the surface. (Any significant decrease in volume due to trapped air should be fixed when the ROV system returns to deck.) Also, as the system descends to depth, volume may decrease slightly due to lower temperatures and higher pressure. Some circuits, like the ROV HPU electric motor compensator, may show increased volume as the oil warms up.

1. Tap INDEX ALL while the TMS and ROV are at the surface, and then monitor level changes during the first 30 meters (100 feet) of descent. Look for unusual compensation volume changes that could be caused by a serious leak or excess air in a compensation circuit.
2. Tap INDEX ALL again at 30 meters (100 feet), when air compression has leveled off. This provides a stable reference for monitoring compensation levels during the rest of the descent.
3. Once the ROV system reaches working depth, compensator and reservoir volumes should stabilize (indicated by the graph lines leveling out). When this occurs, tap INDEX ALL.
