Viewing and Graphing Compensator/Reservoir Volume

See Also for links to information about compensator/reservoir monitoring.

Tap the FLIGHT page COMPS button to view volume levels for compensators and reservoirs. The figure below shows a standard (unexpanded) COMPS panel.

You can expand the COMPS panel to set volume alerts and to indicate whether compensator’s or reservoir’s volume should be graphed.

In the standard and expanded COMPENSATOR & RESERVOIR LEVELS panel, the color of compensator fill identifies the volume level:

● Blue = volume is within the low-level and high-level alarm settings.
● Red = volume is below the low-level alarm setting.
● Orange = volume is above the high-level alarm setting.

The expanded COMPENSATOR & RESERVOIR LEVELS panel lets you graph the volume history of one or more compensators. If no boxes are checked, all compensators are graphed. If at least one box is checked, only those compensators with checked boxes are graphed.

The color of a compensator's check box matches the color of the graphed line.

Estimated Time Until Recovery

Estimated Time Until Recovery feature (at the bottom of the COMPENSATOR & RESERVOIR LEVELS panel) monitors the volume of all compensators and reservoirs. If one or more compensators or reservoirs is losing fluid, Estimated Time Until Recovery displays the time remaining until the item with the fastest fluid loss reaches its minimum acceptable volume. Use the Estimated Time Until Recovery display to determine the in-water time remaining before the system must be recovered to deck replenish compensators or reservoirs (to avoid water intrusion and possible component failure).

Note:  When you display the COMPENSATOR & RESERVOIR LEVELS panel, a value may not immediately display in the "Estimated Time Until Recovery" panel. This is normal because the system requires time to gather the data to accurately predict the rate of fluid loss.
