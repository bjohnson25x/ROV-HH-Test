Flight in Inertial Coordinates and Body Coordinates

See Also for links to general information about ROV flight.

In the default flight mode, the ROV flies in inertial coordinates. This means that the ROV responds to flight joystick movements with flight that follows X, Y, and Z (horizontal and vertical) inertial coordinates, regardless of the vehicle trim (pitch or roll).

When the ROV is using BOTTOM MODE, it flies in body coordinates, which means that the vehicle trim affects the ROV response to flight joystick movements.

Example of movement in inertial coordinates

If the vehicle is pitched down 10 degrees at the front because of a load, forward joystick movement results in true horizontal travel (see the figure below, left panel) because the control software runs vertical thrusters to compensate for the pitch, which otherwise would force the vehicle down. Likewise, when the vehicle is rolled to port or starboard, commands for vertical movement may use lateral thrusters as well as vertical thrusters to achieve true vertical movement.

Example of movement in body coordinates

If the vehicle is pitched down 10 degrees, forward joystick movement causes the vehicle to move forward and down (see the figure above, right panel).

Note:  When BOTTOM MODE is ON, the control system does not use the vertical thrusters to compensate for ROV motion due to pitch. However, even if BOTTOM MODE is on, vertical thrusters are still used if the operator commands vertical movement. Also, if AUTO DEPTH or AUTO ALTITUDE is being used, vertical thrusters run as necessary to maintain the set point.
