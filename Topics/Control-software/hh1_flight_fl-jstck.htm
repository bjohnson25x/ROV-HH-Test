Moving the Joystick to Command Thrust

When you move the flight joystick to command thrust, the control is proportional; this means that the farther from the neutral (center) position that you move the joystick, the greater the thrust. When you return the joystick to its neutral position, the thrusters stop. (When the ROV is operating in its default control mode, momentum can cause the vehicle to continue moving after thrusters stop.)

Moving the Flight Joystick to Command Thrust
Move the Joystick Like this... To Produce this ROV Movement
Push forward (away from you) Vehicle motion forward
Pull back (toward you) Vehicle motion aft
Left Lateral vehicle motion to port
Right Lateral vehicle motion to starboard
Twist counterclockwise Vehicle yaw left
Twist clockwise Vehicle yaw right

Applying Vertical Thrust with the Joystick

Use the flight joystick’s vertical thrust control (shown below) to apply thrust up or down. The farther from the neutral (center) position that you move the control, the greater the vertical thrust.

Moving the vertical thrust control has different effects depending on how you have configured the control.

See Configuring Vertical Thrust Control on the Flight Joystick for more information.

Canceling All Auto Functions

Press the flight joystick’s red switch to cancel all currently active auto functions.
