Viewing and Overriding HPU Shutdown Limits

See Also for links to information about operating and controlling hydraulic circuits.

Caution!  Overriding system safety limits may cause serious damage to the HPU.

The control system automatically turns off electrical power to the HPU motor if any of the following safety limits are exceeded:

● Motor temperature is greater than 80 °C (176 °F).
● Oil temperature is greater than 80 °C (176 °F).
● Oil volume is less than 0.44 liters (0.12 gal) for HD and UHD-III; less than 0.55 liters (0.15 gal) for UHD-II.

On each graph in the expanded HYDRAULIC PRESSURE CONTROL panel, the automatic shutdown level is displayed as a red bar.

You can override the automatic HPU shutdown if:

● You know that the shutdown was caused by a faulty HPU sensor that is reporting inaccurate information.
● You must continue emergency operation of the HPU even though a legitimate automatic shutdown has occurred.

Note:  You must be operating with supervisor permissions to override an automatic HPU shutdown.

To override an automatic HPU shutdown, tap the OVERRIDE button located below the graph that shows the over-limit reading. Then drag the arrow to the unlock icon to perform the override. The override allows the HPU to be restarted for emergency full-pressure operation.
