About FLIGHT Screen Hydraulic Circuit Control

See Also for links to information about operating and controlling hydraulic circuits.

Purpose:

● Adjusts hydraulic circuit pressure set points, and for some systems, flow rate set point.
● Displays HPU status information (like motor temperature and oil volume).
● Adjusts alarm set points for each value.
● Allows override of shutdown conditions.
Access: On the FLIGHT screen, tap HPU.

The type of hydraulic power unit (HPU) on the ROV can vary according to the vehicle's power need and the requirement for auxiliary fluid circuits. An ROV system may be configured to have:

● The HD/UHD-II hydraulic configuration, with (1) one HPU pump powering the main hydraulic circuit and (2) a second HPU pump powering an auxiliary (aux) circuit.
● The UHD-III hydraulic configuration, with (1) two HPU pumps powering the main hydraulic circuit and (2) an ISOL-8 pump for aux hydraulics.

The ISOL-8 pump has two models:

● Eight-cylinder model, which pumps fluid for two auxiliary circuits:
— Aux circuit 1 - uses six cylinders and has its own fluid reservoir.
— Aux circuit 2 - uses two cylinders and has its own fluid reservoir.
● Four-cylinder model, with a single hydraulic circuit (aux circuit 1) and its own fluid reservoir.

Screen Controls for the HD Hydraulic Configuration

In the HYDRAULIC PRESSURE CONTROL panel’s standard (unexpanded) view, you can change circuit pressure and view circuit status information.

In the HYDRAULIC PRESSURE CONTROL panel’s expanded view, you can view graphs of HPU status over time and override automatic shutdown processes.

Viewing and Changing HPU Alarm Levels

In the expanded HYDRAULIC PRESSURE CONTROL panel for the HPU main circuit, you can change the user-set alarm levels for motor temperature, oil temperature, and oil volume. When you tap the desired graph, a slider displays showing a range of values for the alarm level. Drag the slider to the desired value.

Sensor Power and Diagnostics

Each graph in the expanded HPU panel displays the output and history for an HPU sensor (for example, an oil volume sensor). To turn a sensor on/off or begin diagnostics, long press the sensor's graph.

Screen Controls for the UHD-III Hydraulic Configuration

Controls in the HYDRAULIC PRESSURE CONTROL panel vary depending on whether you are viewing information for the main or an aux hydraulic circuit.

Main Hydraulic Circuit Overview

In the HYDRAULIC PRESSURE CONTROL panel’s standard (unexpanded) view for the main hydraulic circuit, you can you can drag the slider to change the main circuit pressure. You can also view circuit status information.

In the HYDRAULIC PRESSURE CONTROL panel’s expanded view, you can you can view graphs of HPU status over time (up to 6 hours), and you can override automatic shutdown processes.

Aux Hydraulic Circuit Overview

In the HYDRAULIC PRESSURE CONTROL panel’s standard (unexpanded) view for aux circuits, you can enable circuit operation and drag the slider to change the circuit pressure and the flow rate. You can also view circuit status information.

Note:  You can set hydraulic flow for an aux hydraulic circuit only if that circuit is configured for flow control. For information on configuring an aux circuit for flow control, see ISOL-8 Pump Device Configuration Properties.

To set the pressure and/or flow rate for an aux circuit, tap the tab for the circuit and then drag the desired slider.

Maximum allowed pressure for normal operation of the auxiliary hydraulic circuits is 211 bars (3000 psi). However, you can override this limit to increase pressure up to 352 bars (5000 psi). You must be operating with supervisor permissions to override the pressure limit, and the override button is available only in the expanded panel of the circuit’s HYDRAULIC PRESSURE CONTROL panel.

Caution!  Operating an ISOL-8 pump hydraulic circuit at pressures above 211 bars (3000 psi) may significantly reduce the life of ISOL-8 pump cylinders.

In the HYDRAULIC PRESSURE CONTROL panel’s expanded view, you can:

● Enable circuit operation.
● Prime the circuit’s pump (required if air has been introduced into the hydraulic circuit). The priming function involves only the cylinders in the circuit being primed. Priming a circuit’s pump can take several minutes, and you cannot prime more than one circuit at a time.
● Set the pressure and/or flow rate and view pressure and flow sensor feedback. The flow slider appears only if you have configured the circuit for flow control (see ISOL-8 Pump Device Configuration Properties).
● Override the pressure limitation of 211 bars (3000 psi) to allow pressures up to 352 bars (5000 psi).
● For circuits that have a reservoir volume sensor, view graphs of the circuit’s reservoir volume over time (up to 6 hours) and override automatic shutdown for low volume.

Viewing and Changing Hydraulic Circuit Alarm Levels

In the expanded HYDRAULIC PRESSURE CONTROL panel for the HPU main circuit, you can change the user-set alarm levels for motor temperature, oil temperature, and oil volume. When you tap the desired graph, a slider displays showing a range of values for the alarm level. Drag the slider to the desired value.

For an aux circuit, a graph for oil volume appears if that circuit has a sensor for reservoir volume. If an oil volume graph appears, you can tap the graph to display a slider showing the range of values for the alarm level. Drag the slider to the desired value.

Sensor Power and Diagnostics

Each graph in the expanded HPU panel displays the output and history for an HPU sensor (for example, an oil volume sensor). To turn a sensor on/off or begin diagnostics, long press the sensor's graph.
