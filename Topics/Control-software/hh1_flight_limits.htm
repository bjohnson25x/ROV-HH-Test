Automatic Performance Limits: Power and Thruster Limiting

See Also for links to general information about ROV flight.

To prevent damage to the ROV’s HPU and thrusters, the control system automatically limits commands for ROV HPU horsepower and thruster rpm.

Power Limiting

The ROV control system is able to operate all thrusters simultaneously and fly the ROV in all three dimensions. But because hydraulic power for the thrusters comes from a fixed supply, full application of hydraulic power to all thrusters in multidimensional flight may result in maximum horsepower being demanded from the HPU. When this occurs, the ROV control system’s power limiting function prevents more than the maximum rated horsepower from being delivered, thus preventing damage to the HPU motor, long line, etc. The power limiting function operates by reducing hydraulic circuit pressure. When power limiting is being applied, the ROV may fly at slower speeds than when fewer thrusters are applied to flight in a single dimension.

Once the demanded horsepower from the HPU drops below the allowed maximum, the control system allows hydraulic circuit pressure to gradually increase to normal.

The dashboard display (see Dashboard Power Data) contains a gauge showing power limiting information.

Thruster Limiting

Thruster limiting prevents more than the rated maximum rpm from being delivered by any thruster. If any thruster begins to exceed the maximum rpm, the system reduces commands to all thrusters to prevent damage. The ROV continues to follow the commanded vector, but at reduced speed.

If the control system is applying thruster limiting, affected thrusters on the FLIGHT screen ROV icon turn orange.
