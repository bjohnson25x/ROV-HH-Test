Power Control for HPU and Instruments

See Also for links to information about controlling ROV and TMS power circuits.

Purpose: Controls ROV and TMS power circuits. Lets you:
●View information about each circuit.
●Turn circuit power on and off.
●Silence the PDU buzzer. Tap SILENCE ALARM to silence PDU warning buzzers for current and future alarms.
Access: On the FLIGHT screen or any TOOLING screen, tap POWER at the bottom of the screen.

Caution!  When the ROV system is on deck, limit HPU operation to prevent overheating, which can damage the HPU.

The FLIGHT screen standard POWER MANAGEMENT AND CONTROL panel shows power circuits for the ROV and TMS. The number of buttons in the panel varies between systems that have different power configurations.

Power Controls for the HD and UHD-III

The standard (unexpanded) POWER MANAGEMENT AND CONTROL panel lets you turn on/off power to individual electrical circuits. If an IPDS contains a Multi Pak unit, you can cycle power to circuits for that Multi Pak using buttons on the panel.

The expanded panel lets you control electrical circuit power and also displays status information for each circuit.

You must turn on the ROV instrument power circuit before you can turn on the ROV HPU circuit.

When you turn on an IPDS, the 48 VDC circuit automatically turns on, but 120 VAC circuits do not.

For information about the differences between main and clean power, see About the ROV Power Subsystem Diagnostics Display.

Power Controls for the UHD-II

The standard (unexpanded) POWER MANAGEMENT AND CONTROL panel lets you turn on/off power to individual electrical circuits.

The expanded panel lets you control electrical circuit power and also displays status information for each circuit.

You must turn on the ROV instrument power circuit before you can turn on the ROV HPU circuit. Also, you must turn on TMS instrument power circuit before you can turn on the TMS HPU circuit.

For information about the differences between main and clean power, see About the ROV Power Subsystem Diagnostics Display.
