About TMS Control

See Also for links to information about operating and controlling the TMS.

Purpose: The TMS CONTROL panel lets you:

● View TMS status information
● Enable/disable TMS control
● Control latches for docking and undocking.

Access: : On the FLIGHT screen, tap TMS CONTROL.

The FLIGHT screen controls, the foot pedal, and the flight joystick can all be used for some aspects of TMS control.

To accurately control tether and drum functions, you must enter correct information (like total tether length) when you configure the tether (About Configuring TMS Tether Settings).

The MASTER OVERRIDE function lets you perform TMS actions that the control system normally does not allow. In unusual conditions, you may need to override normal TMS control restrictions (for example, to pay out tether when the ROV is docked) even at the risk of damaging equipment. For more information, see Performing TMS Override Functions.

For the UHD-II ROV system, a depth interlock prevents the ROV from being unlatched from the TMS until the ROV is submerged to 5 meters or more. For this reason, you must use the MASTER OVERRIDE function to perform TMS operations on deck (including separating the ROV and TMS on deck).

Note:  Be sure to turn off power to the TMS after recovering the ROV system to deck or after performing deck spooling operations.
