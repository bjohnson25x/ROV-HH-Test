Screen Displays for Latching/Unlatching the ROV and TMS

See Also for links to information about operating and controlling the TMS.

The FLIGHT screen TMS CONTROL panel contains displays and controls for latching and unlatching the ROV and TMS.

See general information on latches for HD and UHD-III

HD/UHD-III Latches

The ROV is secured to the TMS with a two-stage (primary-secondary) latching assembly, with two primary latches and two secondary latches. An electric solenoid actuator controls each latch. When solenoid actuators are energized, the latches are pulled open. When the actuators are de-energized, springs push the latches closed.

Both secondary and primary latches are pushed open by the bullet as it enters the latch cage. During docking, the bullet passes first through the secondary latches and then through the primary latches.

The latch button is not available if:

● You are not paying out tether (unless you are operating in MASTER OVERRIDE mode).
● Latches or their sensors are not working properly.

For the HD and UHD-III ROV systems, if a latch has not been calibrated or is out of calibration, a warning message displays on the TMS icon.

See general information on latches for the UHD-II

UHD-II Latches and Sensors

The ROV is secured to the TMS with two primary latches and two secondary latches.

● The primary latches are responsible for latching (lifting and securing) the ROV bullet when the ROV is docked. They are also responsible for unlatching (releasing the ROV for flight).
● The secondary latches provide backup if the primary latches fail.

All latches are hydraulically powered and have sensors that provide latch position feedback to the ROV control system. Sensors also provide position feedback for the height of the ROV bullet in the latch cage.

Primary latches require power to open and close. Secondary latches require power to open, but are spring-loaded to close when not powered.

All latches are controlled by the hydraulic latch manifold.

UHD-II Latch Controls

On the TMS CONTROL panel, the LATCH and UNLATCH buttons control the primary (uppermost) latch pair in the latch assembly.

The UNLATCH button is available if all of the following conditions have been met:

● The TMS CONTROL button is active.
● Latches are closed.
● Tether is being commanded to be paid in or paid out.
● The ROV is submerged to at least 5 meters.

However, even if these conditions have not been met, the UNLATCH button is available if you are operating in TMS MASTER OVERRIDE (see below).

The secondary (lower) latches do not have controls in the TMS CONTROL panel. Instead, the secondary latches are spring-loaded to be closed unless they are actively commanded to open. The latches are automatically commanded to open when a command is given for tether pay-out.

For emergency or diagnostic purposes, you can use the TMS MASTER OVERRIDE function to directly control latching and unlatching. You can also override safety and control interlocks for latching and unlatching.

WARNING!  Using the UNLATCH function when TMS MASTER OVERRIDE is in force can cause the ROV to unexpectedly separate from the TMS, potentially resulting in death, injury, and/or equipment loss. Use good judgment and follow all appropriate safety policies when you operate TMS latches in MASTER OVERRIDE.

Note:  When you are undocking the ROV from the TMS, commanding tether pay-out causes the secondary latches to automatically open. However, no tether is actually paid out until the primary latches are fully open (unless TMS MASTER OVERRIDE is in force). This happens because the ROV control system disables TMS drum and pinch wheel motors until the primary latches are open, keeping tether on the drum until the ROV bullet is free to exit from the primary latch assembly.

Screen Data

Screen indicators provide information on latch and bullet status.

Latch icon color indicates the latch state:

● Yellow = latch is open
● White = latch closed
● Green = latch set (primary or secondary) is closed with the bullet in
● Orange = latch is in transition (for example, between open and closed)
● Orange triangle = latch problem (for example, no communication with the latch, no latch power, or mechanical latch problem)

When the ROV and TMS are safely docked, all latches and the bullet icon are green.

WARNING!  DO NOT LIFT the TMS and ROV unless all latch and bullet symbols on the TMS CONTROL are green. If not all symbols are green, the ROV and TMS are NOT fully docked and are NOT safe to lift.

You should also visually confirm (using the TMS docking camera) that the bullet is safely latched.
