Hardware and Data Routing for HDEVS Video

See Also for links to information about video functions and devices for HDEVS.

This information applies only to ROV systems that have HDEVS rather than TAVS.

HDEVS Hardware Connections

Primary HDEVS hardware modules are:

● HD cameras that output Ethernet signals (typically, the Schilling HD Ethernet camera) and attach to node NIMs. Each HD camera simultaneously outputs an HD video stream and an SD video stream. You can choose whether to view and/or record the HD stream or the SD stream.
● Cameras that output composite analog video signals (typically, SD cameras that output signals in NTSC or PAL format). Signals are converted to Ethernet in a subsea node SDVIM.
● Standard DTS communications hardware.
● Decoder modules that convert Ethernet signals to video signals for display. Typically, a decoder is mounted on the back of a monitor. Each decoder has two output connectors:
— Connector for monitors with an HDMI interface (HDMI 1080p60).
— Connector for monitors with an SD composite analog video interface (BNC connector), NTSC or PAL.
● An SD video topside encoder, which can input up to four video streams from SD deck cameras. Output from a SD deck camera is treated in the same way as subsea SD cameras (routed to a decoder for viewing or recording).
● A video computer that creates overlays for and records SD and HD video (see About the HDEVS Video Computer App).

Video Data Flow for Subsea Cameras

1. A camera captures video and sends it to an interface module connected to a node.
— For an HD camera, video signals are converted to Ethernet signals in the camera, and are then sent to a NIM.
— For an SD camera, composite video signals are sent to an SDVIM, where they are converted to Ethernet signals.
2. From the interface module, signals travel through the DTS system (from subsea node to a rack node) to a decoder attached to a monitor. The monitor can be on the monitor wall or some other location (like the vessel’s bridge).
3. The decoder converts Ethernet signals back into video and also multiplexes any overlays onto the video. (Overlays come from the video computer.)
4. Signals go from the decoder to the monitor for display.

Output from a single camera can be simultaneously routed to multiple sources, including monitors and the video computer for recording.

Any monitor with a decoder can be divided into four separate video displays (quad tiling). Monitors with quad tiling can display only SD video. If you want to display output from an HD camera in a quad-tiled display, you must select the SD video stream from the HD camera.

Overlay Data Flow

An overlay can be a graphic image (like a logo) or a data stream (like heading information from the control system). Overlay information is routed from the video computer through a rack node to the decoder for multiplexing onto the video.

Recording Data Flow

Video signals are routed from the rack node to the video computer for recording.
