About ROV Video Systems

See Also for links to information about video functions and devices for HDEVS.

See Also for links to information about video functions and devices for TAVS.

The video system lets you view, overlay, record, and export video streams from all cameras installed on the ROV system (ROV, TMS, and tool skid) as well as topside cameras (like deck cameras). The FLIGHT screen VIDEO panel lets you route video streams to monitors on the monitor wall or to external monitors (for example, on the vessel’s bridge).

The ROV system is equipped with either:

● HDEVS (high-definition Ethernet video system). If the ROV video system includes a video computer, the ROV is using HDEVS.
● TAVS (tunneled analog video system). If the ROV video system includes a video switcher box to route video inputs and outputs, the ROV is using TAVS.

The video systems differ in:

● Techniques for routing, overlaying, recording, and quad tiling.
— In TAVS, these functions are performed by third-party devices, some with their own user interfaces and software.
— In HDEVS, these functions are performed on ROV control system hardware and are mostly implemented in software. The only user interfaces are in the ROV control system and the Video Computer App.
● Methods of connection to the control system.
— In TAVS, different connection paths are needed for SD cameras than for HD cameras. The HD cameras need additional equipment for connection to the ROV, for display, and for other processing functions, so they do not connect through the DTS.
— In HDEVS, SD cameras and HD cameras use the same routing path through the DTS. No additional connection or processing hardware is required.

In both TAVS and HDEVS, you route video streams for display using the FLIGHT screen VIDEO panel.
