VIDEO Panel Display for TAVS

This information applies only to ROV systems that have TAVS rather than HDEVS.

In TAVS, video streams from system cameras are sent topside so that the control system can display them on the monitor wall. You can also direct video streams to user-accessible ports to export them for recording or display.

For information on video collected from a survey junction box (optional), see the section on the survey junction box in Drawings and Part Lists.

Note:  You must map a video signal to a display through the video switcher (see Configuring the Switcher for TAVS Video).

Configuring Video Displays and Clients

The standard (unexpanded) VIDEO DISPLAY panel shows the available video displays (monitors) and the video stream being routed to each. Tap the video display (or, for quad-tiled monitors, the portion of the video display) that you want to route video to. Select the video source to display from the list of available options.

Note:  Only devices connected to the video switcher can be controlled using the VIDEO DISPLAY panel (see Configuring the Switcher for TAVS Video).

The expanded VIDEO DISPLAY panel has two sections, one for video displays (monitors) and one for video clients (non-monitor video outputs like annotators and recorders). To route video to a video client, tap the desired video client and select from the list of available items.

Note:  A warning is displayed when a video switcher port has lost communications. If this happens, video input from that switch may not be available.

Video clients are shown in the order that they were added to the control system, not in numerical or alphabetical order.

Order of Monitors in the VIDEO Panel

Monitors are arranged in the FLIGHT screen VIDEO panel according to their labels. The labels are displayed in order, starting with numbers and proceeding with letters. For example, if the ROV monitor wall has nine monitors with labels going from 1 through 9, the VIDEO panel’s display section will show monitors 1 through 3 on the top row from left to right, monitors 4-6 on the second row, and monitors 7-9 on the bottom row.

If you add additional displays, and their labels start with letters instead of numbers, those displays will appear in alphabetical order below the displays with number labels. For example, if you add a display labeled “Bridge 2,” it appears below the numbered monitors.

Hard-Wired Monitors on the Monitor Wall

Some monitors on the monitor wall are hard-wired for specific inputs. In a nine-monitor wall:

● Quad-tiled monitors on the top left and top right, (typically named 1-TILER and 3-TILER).
● Sonar appears in the middle center (typically named 5-SONAR).
● System dashboard data appears on the in the bottom center (typically named 8-DASHBOARD).

Ensure that your naming convention for monitors allows these hard-wired monitors to remain in the appropriate places in the FLIGHT screen VIDEO panel.

VIDEO Panel Display for HDEVS

This information applies only to ROV systems that have HDEVS rather than TAVS.

All video streams from ROV system cameras are available for display on either the monitor wall or external monitors (for example, a monitor on the vessel's bridge). These video streams are also available for recording on a video computer. The FLIGHT screen VIDEO panel shows video display locations and recording assignments.

Each monitor on the monitor wall (except for the monitors hardwired to display the control system dashboard and sonar data) is connected to a decoder module that processes video signals for that monitor. Each decoder module is installed as a separate device and is listed in the CAMERAS section of installed devices. You must configure each decoder module to indicate its position on the monitor wall (see Video Decoder Configuration Properties).

Note:  For information on which monitor positions are typically hardwired to display the ROV control system dashboard and the sonar data display, see About the Control Console Monitors.

If an area on the VIDEO panel does not show a monitor, a decoder module has not been configured to display on that monitor. By default, decoders that are not assigned to a place on the monitor wall display in the off-wall tab of the VIDEO panel.

Any monitor that receives input through a decoder module can be divided into four separate displays (called quad tiling), letting one monitor receive up to four video streams.

Monitors with quad tiling can display only SD video. If you want to display output from an HD camera in a quad-tiled monitor, you must select the SD video stream from that HD camera.

The VIDEO panel has a tab for each video computer. To change the label on the tab, change the label for the video computer (using the configuration screen for the video computer).

Configuring a Monitor for Quad Tiling

Any monitor with a decoder can be configured for quad tiling.

See how
1. On the FLIGHT screen or the TOOLING page, tap VIDEO at the bottom of the screen.
2. When the VIDEO panel displays, tap the desired tab (MONITOR WALL or OFF WALL).
3. Locate the desired monitor, and tap the drop-down list below that monitor.
4. Tap QUAD TILING.

Routing a Video Stream to a Monitor

In the FLIGHT page VIDEO panel, you can route the video stream from any camera installed in the ROV system to any monitor that contains a decoder. You can also add or remove an overlay for that video.

See how
1. On the FLIGHT screen or the TOOLING page, tap VIDEO at the bottom of the screen.
2. When the VIDEO panel displays, tap the MONITOR WALL tab (to route a video stream to a monitor on the monitor wall) or OFF WALL (to route video to a monitor not on the monitor wall).
3. Locate the desired monitor and display the drop-down list of available video streams. If the monitor is not quad tiled, the drop-down list displays both HD and SD video streams. If the monitor is quad tiled, the drop-down list displays only SD video streams.

4. Select the desired video stream. (You can display same video stream on more than one monitor.)
5. If you want to use an overlay for the video stream, turn on the overlay option in the drop-down list below the monitor. (If you want to change the format of the current overlay, you must use the Video Computer App.)

Selecting Video Streams for Recording

Each video computer in the ROV system has a tab in the FLIGHT screen VIDEO panel. You can use this panel to select video streams to record and to turn an overlay for that video stream on or off.

When you select a video stream in the VIDEO PC tab of the VIDEO panel, that stream begins to record.

See how

A single video computer can simultaneously record one of these combinations:

● Two HD video streams
● One HD video stream and two SD streams
● Four SD streams

To configure the number and quality of video streams, use the Video Computer App to configure video type (see Configuring Recording Details and Viewing Overlays).

Each video computer has a tab in the FLIGHT screen VIDEO panel, and this tab displays a box for each video stream that you can record on that video computer. In the example below, the video computer has been configured to record one HD stream and two SD streams, so the display has three boxes, one with “HD” in the upper right corner and two with “SD” in the upper right corner.

For each box, you can use the drop-down list to select a video stream to record. The list will contain only video streams of the right quality (for example, a box with the HD video quality indicator will show only HD video streams in its drop-down list).

Use the additional drop-down list below each video box to:

● Turn an overlay on or off for that video stream.
● Start and stop client recording for that video stream.

You can also use the Video Computer App to select video streams to record, and to turn the overlay on or off (see About Recording Video with the Video Computer). If you use the Video Computer App to select new video streams for recording, those changes automatically appear in the FLIGHT screen VIDEO panel. If you use the FLIGHT screen VIDEO panel to select new video streams for recording, those changes automatically appear in the Video Computer App interface.

Starting and Stopping Client Recording

For any video stream that is being routed to the video computer for recording, you can start and stop client recording for the video stream. Client recording requires that you connect an external memory device to the video computer and enable that device for client recording (see Configuring for Client Recording).

General Steps for Installing a Camera (HDEVS Only)

Because you must (1) configure a decoder module for a particular monitor and also (2) route a camera’s output to a particular decoder module, you should follow a general sequence of events when you install a camera.

See how
1. Follow the instructions for installing a preconfigured device (see Installing a Preconfigured Device) to install the camera.
2. The monitor that you want to route to should already have an installed decoder. If not, install the decoder and assign it to that monitor (see Video Decoder Configuration Properties).
3. In the FLIGHT screen VIDEO panel, locate the desired monitor.
4. Tap the drop-down list for that monitor and select the camera. The video stream for that camera is then directed to that monitor.
