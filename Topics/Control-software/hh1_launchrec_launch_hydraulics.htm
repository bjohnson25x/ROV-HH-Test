Hydraulics: Launch Procedures

See Also for links to information about launching and recovering the ROV system.

WARNING!  Use caution when you perform ROV deck operations, especially when people are nearby. Clear people from the area during thruster testing, manipulator operation, pan and tilt operation, or when high voltage is applied to the system.

HPU Operation

Caution!  Heat generated by extended on-deck operation of ROV or TMS equipment can damage the equipment and related components. During on-deck operations, do not override temperature safety limits set in the ROV control system software. Overriding safety limits can result in component damage or failure. When equipment is running in air, do not touch the equipment to test temperature.

Turn power on and off to the ROV HPU (and, if this system uses a hydraulic TMS, the TMS HPU) to confirm operation. You can adjust pressure with the slider(s) on the right side of the screen. Use the screen functions only to confirm HPU operation; do not leave the HPU motor running when the HPU is on deck.

Manipulators

Test for proper operation of manipulators and for the setup of the control assignments. If the ROV HPU is not already on, tap the HPU button at the bottom right of the screen and adjust the operating pressure. You can adjust valve pack pressures using the sliders on the right side of the screen. You can adjust control assignments with the buttons to the right of each manipulator image.

ROV Thruster Check

Confirm proper operation of thrusters by commanding ROV flight:

1. Enable and then disable ROV flight with the ROV FLIGHT button.
2. Enable the ROV HPU and set the pressure at minimum.
3. Enable ROV flight, confirm that flight joystick control is assigned to the desired control station, and then use the flight joystick to operate the thrusters.
4. Observe the diagram on the screen to verify thruster spin direction and velocity.
5. When you have finished operating the thrusters, turn off the ROV HPU.

TMS Operation

Caution!  When the TMS and ROV are docked, pay out the smallest amount of tether possible when you are deck testing TMS functions. Paying out too much length can damage the tether.

Test TMS operation by enabling TMS operation and then commanding tether pay-in and pay-out. (You must use the TMS MASTER OVERRIDE function to pay out tether when the ROV is docked, as described in Performing TMS Override Functions.) You need to pay in and pay out only a small amount of tether to test tether movement.

Prelaunch

Just before the ROV is submerged:

● Zero the depth sensors.
● Reset the tether turn counter to zero.
● Reset the umbilical turn counter to zero.
