Instrument Power

WARNING!  Use caution when you perform ROV deck operations, especially when people are nearby. Clear people from the area during thruster testing, manipulator operation, pan and tilt operation, or when high voltage is applied to the system.

Turn on instrument power for the ROV and TMS. Confirm that:

● Nodes are communicating
● Fiber light levels are acceptable
● RSTP is operating

Device Power and Line Isolation

WARNING!  Use caution when you perform ROV deck operations, especially when people are nearby. Clear people from the area during thruster testing, manipulator operation, pan and tilt operation, or when high voltage is applied to the system.

Confirm that devices are powered on and communicating properly. Use the device power drop-down menu for each platform to cycle power to individual devices. Devices with communication issues are listed for each platform and indicated by an orange triangle. Tap the blue arrow next to each issue notification to go directly to that device's diagnostics page.

Line isolation values for each platform and circuit are displayed to the right of the device statuses. A higher reading on the meter corresponds to a higher line resistance.

See an illustration

This illustration shows the screen for an HD or UHD-III; the screen for a UHD-II would show power controls and line isolation data for an ROV system that uses an IPDS rather than an IPA.

For circuits that are not powered, line isolation bars are gray. Color coding for the line isolation bars means:

● Green = good level of resistance
● Orange = questionable level of resistance
● Red = possible fault

Compensators

Compensators are displayed and grouped by the platform they are installed on. Confirm that the compensator fill and alarm levels are correct. You can adjust individual alarm levels by tapping the compensator icon that you wish to adjust. Use INDEX ALL to automatically set alarm levels for all compensators.

Note:  The notification on this screen that some compensator volumes are low is advisory only and does not prevent you from launching the ROV system.

Cameras and Pan and Tilts

WARNING!  Use caution when you perform ROV deck operations, especially when people are nearby. Clear people from the area during thruster testing, manipulator operation, pan and tilt operation, or when high voltage is applied to the system.

Cameras and pan and tilts are listed according to the platform they are installed on. Pan and tilt control is available for testing each function. You can cycle pan and tilt power separately by tapping the pan and tilt’s button. Icons representing camera power, focus type (auto/manual), pan function communication, tilt function communication, and camera calibration are displayed on the right of the screen.

Lights

WARNING!  Do not touch lights when they are on or have been used recently; the lights they may be extremely hot.

Caution!  Leave lights on for only a brief time when the equipment is on deck. (Consult the manufacturer for specific recommendations.) Since lights designed for subsea use are meant to be cooled by surrounding water, running lights for too long on deck will damage the lights.

The LIGHTS screen lets you test the function of each light. You can switch lights on and off, and if the lights can be dimmed, you can adjust their brightness.

Use the LIGHTS screen only to test light function. After testing, turn the lights off until the ROV is submerged in water and ready for flight.
