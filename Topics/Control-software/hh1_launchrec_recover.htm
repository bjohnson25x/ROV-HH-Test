On-Screen Recovery Sequence

See Also for links to information about launching and recovering the ROV system.

Purpose: Guides you through preparing the ROV and TMS for ascent and postdive checks.
Access: On the Main Menu, tap LAUNCH AND RECOVERY and then tap RECOVER.

WARNING!  Use caution when you perform ROV deck operations, especially when people are nearby. Clear people from the area during thruster testing, manipulator operation, pan and tilt operation, or when high voltage is applied to the system.

Ascent

Flight Control

Use the FLIGHT screen to monitor ROV depth as the ROV is pulled toward the surface. Use the buttons to assign flight control to either station, or to enable and disable flight control. For systems with a TMS, the screen shows the number of tether turns.

Surface

Lights

WARNING!  Do not touch lights when they are on or have been used recently because they may be extremely hot.

Caution!  Leave lights on for only a brief time when the equipment is on deck. (Consult the manufacturer for specific recommendations.) Since lights designed for subsea use are meant to be cooled by surrounding water, running lights for too long on deck will damage the lights.

Just before the ROV reaches the surface, use the lights screen to confirm that all lights are powered off.

HPU Power and Control

Caution!  Heat generated by extended on-deck operation of ROV or TMS equipment can damage the equipment and related components. During on-deck operations, do not override temperature safety limits set in the ROV control system software. Overriding safety limits can result in component damage or failure. When equipment is running in air, do not touch the equipment to test temperature.

Use the HPU Power and Control screen to return the ROV HPU main and auxiliary circuits to minimum pressure. Turn off power to the circuits when they are no longer needed. If the ROV system has a hydraulic TMS, turn off power to the TMS HPU when it is no longer needed.

Instrument Power

Shut off instrument power after the vehicle is brought on deck and instruments are no longer needed. Ensure that the vehicle is electrically grounded when it is on deck.

Review

Issue Review

The issue review screen displays a list of all alarms and issues that were raised during the dive. This screen lets you review the issues and take corrective action where needed.

Dive Summary

The dive summary screen displays the duration of the dive and the maximum depth achieved. The summary screen also shows a visual representation of the depth over the length of the dive.
