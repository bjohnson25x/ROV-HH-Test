BOP Settings and Estimated Time to Close the BOP

See Also for links to information about BOP intervention.

Based on information that you provide in the BOP SETTINGS panel of the BOP intervention screen, the control system estimates the best-case time and worst-case time to close the BOP. Calculations are based on:

● The pressure setting for the ISOL-8 BOP intervention circuit. (Pressure is set using the pressure slider on the BOP screen.)
● The VOLUME TO CLOSE entry in the BOP SETTINGS panel of the BOP screen.
● The number of cylinders in the ISOL-8 pump BOP intervention circuit. You can configure the number of cylinders as described in Schilling Valve Pack Device Configuration Properties.

If the best-case time is greater than 45 seconds, the control system warns you that the intervention may not be successful. However, the control system does not prevent you from performing the intervention.

For items in the BOP SETTINGS panel of the BOP intervention screen, use the keyboard and/or number pad to enter the desired text and numbers.

For MAX PRESSURE, use ratings for the BOP model and information about the BOP’s condition to determine the desired maximum pressure in the ISOL-8 pump BOP intervention circuit. During an intervention, you can force circuit pressure to 35 bars [500 psi] above this maximum pressure.
