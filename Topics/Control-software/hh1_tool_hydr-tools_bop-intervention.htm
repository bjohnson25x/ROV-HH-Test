Performing the BOP Intervention

See Also for links to information about BOP intervention.

Caution!  Be aware of all information and follow all steps listed or referred to in this section. Failure to consider all steps and to execute them correctly can cause the BOP intervention to fail.

Caution!  Follow all applicable steps and observe all safety precautions in your company’s BOP intervention procedures. Before you begin an intervention, ensure that no instructions in your company’s BOP intervention procedures conflict with procedures listed in the ROV technical manual.

Note:  The user profile for your control station does not need to include supervisor permissions for you to perform a BOP intervention. However, in the course of a BOP intervention, you may need to override an automatic HPU shutdown, which requires supervisor permissions. If you want the ability to override automatic HPU shutdowns, ensure that the user profile for your control station includes supervisor permissions.

Planning and Configuring for the Intervention

● Consider all the issues listed in Planning a BOP Intervention.
● Perform all necessary configuration tasks listed in Configuring for BOP Intervention.

Performing Initial Checks

● Ensure that the PDU is communicating with the control system.
● Ensure that the HPU has power and is communicating with the control system.
● Ensure that the ISOL-8 pump is communicating with the control system.
● Ensure that hot stab control is assigned to a valve pack valve.
● If the valve pack containing the hot stab valve uses a circuit other than the HPU main circuit or the ISOL-8 pump BOP intervention circuit, check to ensure that the circuit is being commanded and has sufficient pressure and flow to operate the hot stab valve.
● Ensure that the valve pack for the hot stab valve has power and is communicating with the control system.
● Ensure that the ISOL-8 pump BOP intervention circuit is disabled. (The control system will automatically enable the circuit when the intervention begins.)

Setting up the BOP Screen

1. Display the BOP intervention screen:
a. On the Main Menu, tap TOOLING.
b. Tap the TOOLS tab on the screen top right.
c. Tap the tab for the BOP intervention tool on the screen top left.
2. When the BOP intervention screen displays, ensure that settings in the BOP SETTINGS panel are correct (see BOP Settings and Estimated Time to Close the BOP).
3. View the best-case and worst-case estimated BOP closure times (described in BOP Settings and Estimated Time to Close the BOP) located beneath the BOP SETTINGS panel. Consider this information as you decide whether to proceed with the intervention.
4. Set pressure for the ISOL-8 pump BOP intervention circuit.

Note:  During normal ISOL-8 pump operation, pressure is limited to 211 bars (3000 psi) unless you override that pressure limitation. You do not need to perform this pressure override during a BOP intervention; you can raise pressure up beyond 211 bars (3000 psi) to as high as 352 bars (5000 psi) simply by moving the slider on the pressure gauge.

5. If you know before the intervention begins that you want to use the seawater intake function, enable seawater intake (see Using Seawater as Pumped Fluid (Seawater Intake)). You can also enable seawater intake at any time during the intervention.

Performing the Intervention

1. Tap BOP CONTROL to enable BOP control. Enabling BOP control does not start fluid pumping; instead, it gives pressure control of the HPU main circuit and the ISOL-8 pump BOP intervention circuit to the BOP process. If some condition prevents BOP control (for example, if the ISOL-8 pump is not communicating with the control system), the BOP CONTROL button does not become enabled and a screen message describes the problem.

Once you have enabled BOP control, the ROV control system prevents you from manually controlling the HPU main circuit and the ISOL-8 pump BOP intervention circuit. (You can still control the ISOL-8 circuit that has not been designated for the BOP intervention.) To regain manual control of the HPU main circuit and the ISOL-8 pump BOP intervention circuit, you must disable BOP control.

2. Tap PERFORM INTERVENTION, and then drag the lock icon to the right. This action simultaneously zeros the fluid volume counter and opens the valve that controls fluid flow to the hot stab, and fluid pumping begins.
3. Observe the chart, which shows the hydraulic circuit pressure and fluid volume pumped. From this information, you can infer the progress of the intervention. Also observe the ELAPSED TIME counter above the chart.
4. If necessary, raise or lower the BOP intervention circuit pressure. During operation, you can raise the pressure to 35 bars (500 psi) above the maximum pressure set in the BOP SETTINGS panel.
5. If necessary, enable seawater intake (see Using Seawater as Pumped Fluid (Seawater Intake)).
6. If you need to stop fluid flow, tap PERFORM INTERVENTION. To restart, tap PERFORM INTERVENTION again. The ELAPSED TIME counter stops while fluid is not flowing, but continues if and when fluid flow starts again.
7. When the intervention is complete, tap PERFORM INTERVENTION to stop fluid flow. Then tap BOP CONTROL to disable BOP control. This action:
— Releases the HPU main circuit and ISOL-8 pump BOP intervention circuit to allow manual control.
— Resets the intervention timer to zero.
— Saves the chart data recorded during the intervention.

Note:  The intervention automatically stops if (1) the HPU has an automatic shutdown or (2) a device involved in the intervention (like the valve commanding fluid flow, the HPU, or the ISOL-8 pump) loses communications with the control system. This means that fluid flow stops, and the HPU and ISOL-8 pump BOP intervention circuit are released from BOP intervention control.

If you want to export chart data for this intervention (or any previous intervention), see Exporting Chart Data for a BOP Intervention.
