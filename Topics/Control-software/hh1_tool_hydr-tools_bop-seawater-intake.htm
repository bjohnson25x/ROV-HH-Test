Using Seawater as Pumped Fluid (Seawater Intake)

See Also for links to information about BOP intervention.

Caution!  Pumping seawater through an ISOL-8 pump circuit will contaminate the circuit and may damage equipment.

Note:  Seawater intake can be used only for a BOP intervention.

The ISOL-8 pump aux circuit 1 includes a seawater suction valve. When you enable seawater intake, the pump first uses the contents of the circuit’s reservoir. If the control system senses that the reservoir cannot provide enough fluid, the seawater suction valve opens, pulling in seawater and providing an unlimited fluid source.

If the ISOL-8 pump has two circuits, and if you are allowing the contents of the two circuits to mix (by configuring the aux circuit combine plate), the ISOL-8 pump will first draw on the reservoirs for both aux circuits before opening to seawater.

Note:   Enabling seawater intake does not necessarily begin seawater intake; the ISOL-8 pump will pump seawater only when the control system senses that not enough fluid is available from the ISOL-8 pump reservoir(s).

You can enable seawater intake before an intervention begins or at any time during the intervention.

To enable seawater intake:

1. On the BOP intervention page, tap SEAWATER INTAKE. The seawater intake panel then displays.

2. Type “seawater” into the text box. (The text can be in upper or lower case.) The SEAWATER INTAKE button then displays the lock icon.

3. Tap SEAWATER INTAKE and drag the lock to the right. The SEAWATER INTAKE button then displays as enabled on the BOP intervention screen.

To disable seawater intake at any time, tap the SEAWATER INTAKE button.
