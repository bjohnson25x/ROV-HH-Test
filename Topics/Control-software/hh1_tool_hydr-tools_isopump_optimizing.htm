Optimizing the ISOL-8 Pump for Tool Operation

See Also for links to information about tool hydraulics.

To operate most tools with the ISOL-8 pump, you can follow the instructions in ISOL-8 Pump Tool Operation in Pressure-Control Mode or ISOL-8 Pump Tool Operation in Flow-Control Mode. However, you may sometimes need to optimize pump operation to:

● Minimize heat buildup in the HPU main circuit.
● Maximize ISOL-8 pump performance.

ISOL-8 Pump Load Limits and HPU Power Management

To optimize ISOL-8 pump performance, you must:

● Understand the concept of pump loads.
● Know how the ROV control system limits current draw from the HPU main circuit pump.

Pump Load Limits

Any restriction in the ISOL-8 pump hydraulic circuit (for example, an operating tool) creates a load on the pump. The ROV control system displays the message “Pump is Load Limited” in the HYDRAULIC PRESSURE CONTROL panel beneath the PRESSURE and FLOW gauges when the pump is working as hard as it can, given the current settings.

The “Pump is Load Limited” message does not necessarily indicate a dangerous or damaging condition. However, the indicator provides important information about the state of ISOL-8 pump operations.

HPU Power Management

The ROV control system monitors the motor line current for the HPU main circuit electric motor. If line current exceeds the control system’s safety threshold, the main hydraulic circuit pressure is automatically lowered to reduce motor current draw. When this power management condition goes into effect, the message POWER MANAGEMENT appears in orange letters near the ROV icon on the FLIGHT screen. The dashboard display (see Dashboard Power Data) also contains a gauge showing power management information.

Once the demand for HPU horsepower drops below the safety threshold, the ROV control system allows the HPU main circuit pressure to gradually increase to normal.

Minimizing Heat in the HPU Main Circuit

When the ISOL-8 pump is operating, excess pressure in the HPU main circuit (that is, more pressure than is needed for the load on the ISOL-8 pump) causes oil in the HPU main circuit to heat up. You can minimize heating by operating the ISOL-8 pump with the minimum pressure required for the pump’s load.

If oil temperature for the HPU main hydraulic circuit is too high or is rising too quickly:

1. Look at the HYDRAULIC PRESSURE CONTROL panel for the ISOL-8 pump circuit being used to operate the tool. If the message “Pump is Load Limited” does not display below the PRESSURE and FLOW gauges, then the pressure on the HPU main circuit is higher than is required for the ISOL-8 pump’s current load.
2. Slowly reduce the pressure setting for the HPU main circuit until the “Pump is Load Limited” indicator displays in the HYDRAULIC PRESSURE CONTROL panel for the ISOL-8 pump circuit. When this happens, the HPU is no longer producing excess pressure.
3. Stop lowering the pressure and observe tool operation.
a. If the tool is performing with acceptable force and speed, continue operation without further adjusting the ISOL-8 pump circuit pressure and flow settings.
b. If tool performance is not acceptable, continue with the next step.
4. Unacceptable tool performance probably means that the HPU main pressure and/or ISOL-8 pump flow are too low to produce the desired output. Slowly raise the HPU main circuit pressure setting until tool performance is acceptable. However, oil in the HPU main circuit may begin to heat up.
a. If the heating is acceptable (based on operating conditions and the expected duration of tool use), continue operation without further adjustments.
b. If the heating is not acceptable, continue with the next step.
5. Operate thrusters at low speed to cool the oil in the HPU main circuit. Once thrusters are operating, you may need to return to step 1 to optimize the HPU main circuit.

Optimizing Pressure and Flow to Improve Performance

Note:  Before you try to optimize performance, ensure that you are not trying to achieve more pressure or flow than the ISOL-8 pump is rated for.

If the ISOL-8 pump is not producing enough pressure or flow for a tool operation, you can try to optimize performance:

1. Check for indicators of pump performance status.
a. In the HYDRAULIC PRESSURE CONTROL panel for the ISOL-8 pump circuit being used to operate the tool, note whether the message “Pump is Load Limited” is displayed below the PRESSURE and FLOW gauges.
b. Note whether the ROV control system is performing power management by checking the area near the ROV icon on the FLIGHT screen or the power data section of the dashboard.
2. Based on the indicators for load limiting and power management, decide whether optimization is possible.
a. If the pump is load limited and power management is being performed, the ISOL-8 pump is at maximum power. Further adjustments will not improve tool performance.
b. If the pump is load limited but power management is not being performed, go to the next step.
3. Slowly raise the HPU main circuit pressure until either the load limit indicator disappears or power management goes into effect. If the load limit message disappears, the ISOL-8 pump is optimized for the highest efficiency (the least production of heat). Do not raise the HPU main circuit pressure unless you are willing to let heat build up in that circuit.

Note:  If you raise the HPU main circuit pressure to its maximum but power management never goes into effect, the ISOL-8 pump has achieved the best possible performance given its current load. Further adjustments will not improve tool performance.
