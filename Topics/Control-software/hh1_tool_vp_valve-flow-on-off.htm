Turning Valve Flow On and Off

See Also for links to information about valve pack control.

On Schilling Robotics valve packs, valves that are not assigned to rate-controlled manipulators or other hydraulic tools can be controlled (turned on and off) individually from the valve pack screen.

Note:  You can assign a valve to a tactile control on the tool control pod, but you cannot use the tactile control and the screen control at the same time.

For information about controlling valves that are assigned to rate-controlled manipulators or other hydraulic tools, see About Controlling Hydraulic Tools.

Before you can control a valve pack valve, you must access the screen for the valve pack.

See how
1. From the Main Menu, tap TOOLING.
2. Tap the VALVES tab in the upper right.
3. Tap the tab in the upper left for the desired valve pack.

If the valve is not already enabled, tap the valve button (the button containing a down arrow) and then tap the DISABLED button. When the button label changes to ENABLED, the valve is ready for operation.

Turning Flow On and Off for Two-Port Valves

Most valve pack valves have two output ports, A and B. To turn on flow, you must pick the desired port.

Turning Flow On and Off for Single-Port Valves

High-flow valve pack valves typically have a single output port. Controls are identical to those for two-port valves except that the button for port A does not display. To operate the valve, use controls for port B.
