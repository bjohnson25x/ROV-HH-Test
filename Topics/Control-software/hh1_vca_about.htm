About the HDEVS Video Computer App

See Also for links to information about the Video Computer App. The Video Computer App is used only with HDEVS.

See Also for links to information about video functions and devices for HDEVS.

Purpose: For both SD (standard-definition) and HD (high-definition) video, the Video Computer App lets you:

● Preview, record, and play back video.
● Record audio annotations.
● Create and manage overlays that can be added to (superimposed over) camera views and recorded video.
● Put place markers in videos to mark the locations of events.
Access: Turn on the video computer, and the Video Computer App automatically starts up.

Note:  The Video Computer App is used only with HDEVS (see About ROV Video Systems).

The Video Computer App, which runs as a stand-alone application on the video computer, lets you preview, overlay, and record selected video streams from the ROV system. You can also add audio annotations to videos and put event place markers in recorded video to mark specific locations.

When you use the Video Computer App to select one or more video streams, the streams are automatically recorded on both of the video computer’s internal drives (hard drives). Simultaneous recording on both drives offers protection if one of the drives fails. When the internal drives become full, the oldest video segments are deleted to make room for new segments. To record significant events from a job, you can also manually record selected segments directly onto external memory devices in a process called client recording.

The user interface for the Video Computer App is typically routed to a monitor on the monitor wall as a KVM-switched source (see About the Control Console Monitors).

A single video computer can simultaneously record one of these combinations:

● Two HD video streams
● One HD video stream and two SD streams
● Four SD streams

If the ROV system needs to record additional video streams, another video computer must be added. Each video computer runs an individual copy of the Video Computer App, and the software for each computer must be individually managed and configured.

Each HD camera simultaneously outputs an HD video stream and an SD video stream. You can choose whether to view and/or record the HD stream or the SD stream.

The VIDEO DASHBOARD is the startup screen, and you can access all Video Computer App functions from controls on that screen.

Starting Up the Video Computer App

1. Ensure that the ROV control system software is running.
2. Turn on the video computer, and the Video Computer App starts up automatically.

If the ROV control system software is not running when you turn on the video computer, the Video Computer App starts up in offline mode (see Video Computer App Offline Mode).

Shutting Down the Video Computer App

From the VIDEO DASHBOARD, click the shutdown icon (see Using the VIDEO DASHBOARD). This shuts down the Video Computer App and turns off the video computer.

Offline Mode

To record video streams from ROV system cameras, the video computer must communicate with computers for the ROV control system (the client computers and the RTC). When the video computer cannot communicate with the control system (for example, because the control system has been restarted), the Video Computer App goes into an offline mode and can no longer preview or record video streams. For information about how to restore the Video Computer App to online status, see Video Computer App Offline Mode.
