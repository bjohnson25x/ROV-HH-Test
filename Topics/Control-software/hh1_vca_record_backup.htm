Performing Continuous Backup Recording

See Also for links to more information about recording on the video computer. This information applies only to ROV systems with HDEVS.

This information applies only to ROV systems that have HDEVS rather than TAVS.

In continuous backup recording, video streams are recorded on both video computer internal drives (hard drives). Simultaneous recording on both drives offers protection if one of the drives fails. When the internal drives become full, the oldest video segments are deleted to make room for new segments.

Starting Continuous Backup Recording for a Video Stream

Note:  For information on starting client recording for a video stream, see Starting and Stopping Client Recording.

1. If necessary, adjust the number and type of video streams being routed to the video computer (see Configuring Recording Details and Viewing Overlays).
2. From the video preview panel, select the drop-down list for the desired pane.
3. When the drop-down list for that pane displays, click the desired video stream.

4. Click a video stream to select it for recording on. (A new selection replaces an old one.) To deselect a video stream and not replace it with another one, click NOT ASSIGNED.
5. Click Enable Overlay to add or remove an overlay (indicated by a check mark) from the video stream. If you add an overlay, it displays in the preview panel for that video stream.

As soon as you select a video stream, the video stream displays in the preview panel and the Video Computer App begins recording it.

Note:  Video streams are available for display and recording for all cameras that are installed in the ROV control system configuration.

To see a list of all installed cameras:

1. Go to any CONFIGURATION screen for the ROV control system (not the Video Computer Appâ€™s VIDEO CONFIGURATION screen).
2. Tap the DEVICES tab.
3. Tap INSTALLED DEVICES.
4. Tap the CAMERAS tab.

Stopping Continuous Backup Recording for a Video Stream

1. From the video preview panel, select the drop-down list for the video stream.
2. When the drop-down list displays on the screen, either click NOT ASSIGNED (to deselect the video stream without replacing it) or click another video stream (to stop recording for the original stream and replace it with recording for a new stream).

Note:  For information on stopping client recording for a video stream, see Starting and Stopping Client Recording. You can stop client recording of a video stream without stopping continuous backup recording of that video stream on the internal drives.
