Overview of Video Computer Recording

See Also for links to information about the Video Computer App. The Video Computer App is used only with HDEVS.

See Also for links to more information in the video recording start-to-finish guide. This guide applies only to ROV systems with HDEVS.

This information applies only to ROV systems that have HDEVS rather than TAVS.

The start-to-finish guide for video recording provides an overview of recording functions from initial configuration to video editing and archiving.

As part of planning an intervention task for the ROV system, you should configure the ROV control software and the Video Computer App for the job’s specific requirements for video recording. Performing the configuration before the job begins will greatly simplify the work of recording, annotating, and exporting video to meet the job’s requirements.

Continuous Video Backup

Whenever you route video from ROV system cameras to the Video Computer App, that video is recorded on both video computer internal drives. (Simultaneous recording on both drives offers protection if one of the drives fails.) The internal drives work in a continuous backup mode; once the drives are full, the oldest video is deleted to make room for the newest video.

Video stored on the internal drives can be exported to an external memory device. Because video segments can be large, exporting many video segments can take a long time. If you know that you will want to export video of certain operations during a job, you can create specific video segments for those operations using client recording. Exporting segments from client recording will typically take a shorter time.

Client Recording

The client recording feature lets you attach an external memory device to the video computer (using a USB 3 port) and copy video segments to that device during recording. These client recordings provide redundant backup of the video on the internal drives, and because they are already on the external memory device, they decrease time needed to copy video files at the end of a job.

● You can start and stop client recording at any time, creating video segments that are shorter than 15 minutes. During client recording, these segments are automatically stored on all external memory devices that have been enabled (see Configuring for Client Recording).
● When you have finished all client recording that you want to accomplish for a recording session, you must finalize video segments on the external memory device. The finalizing process adds audio annotations to the video and creates a video log on the external drive. Do not remove an external memory device until video segments recorded on that device have been finalized.
