HD Node Compensation: Fill, Bleed, and Drain

See Also for links to service instructions related to subsea nodes.

This service instruction explains how to fill, bleed, and drain the node compensation circuits, on HD ROVs without rapid fill and drain systems.

Tools Compensation cart, compressed air supply
Personnel 1 worker
Sub-Procedures None
Parts and Kits None
Safety General shop conditions
Consumables Cable ties, compensation fluid

Filling and Bleeding

1. Ensure that the node is oriented so the compensation line (A) enters at the lowest point of the node and the compensation bleed line/relief valve (B) exits at the highest point.
2. Connect the compensation cart QD to the node compensation QD (A).
3. Orient the relief valve (B) above the node so the valve is the highest point in the circuit. Open the relief valve. The valve can be held open by hand or by inserting the tip of a cable tie.
4. Fill the node by moving the cart selector lever to the FILL position.
5. Close the relief valve when a steady stream of oil is flowing from the relief valve. Leave the compensation cart connected and in the FILL position.

Caution!  Do not set the compensation cart pressure above 1 bar (15 psi). Compensation cart charging pressures above 1 bar (15 psi) will force fluid through the relief valves.

6. Bleed the SeaNet cables:

Note:  Devices compensated by SeaNet cables (such as the rotary actuator) should be filled separately before bleeding the nodes.

a. Start with the SeaNet cables mounted at the highest elevation.
b. Open the bleed screw (C) on the SeaNet connector at the node to remove air in the connector. A small amount of air may be trapped here, while the majority of the air will be spread throughout the cable.
c. Disconnect the SeaNet cable at the device end.
d. Press the poppet valve (D) to bleed air from the SeaNet cable. Continue bleeding the cable until there are no air bubbles coming out with the fluid.
e. Reconnect the SeaNet cable to the device.
f. Repeat the process for the remaining SeaNet cables, moving from the highest to lowest.

7. Bleed the 26V power cable:
a. Leave the 26V power cable connected to the node and disconnect the power cable at the IPDS.
b. Elevate the cable to allow air to rise to the disconnected end.
c. Depress the valve stem protruding from center of the removed connector. Bleed the cable until all air is purged.
d. Reconnect the 26V power cable to the power source.
8. Bleed the modules with blanking caps (if present):
a. Remove the blanking cap.
b. Press on the SeaNet penetrator to allow any trapped air to exit the node.
c. Once there is no more air coming from the penetrator, fill the blanking cap with compensation fluid and install it on the penetrator.
9. Bleed the compensated fiber lines.

Note:  Fiber lines are NOT compensated by the node.

a. Leave the fiber line connected to the IPDS T-box and disconnect the fiber line at the other end.
b. Elevate the cable to allow air to rise to the disconnected end.
c. Press in on the fiber end with a fiber cap. Bleed the cable until all air is purged.

Caution!  Only use a proper ST fiber cap to bleed cables. Never insert other objects.
d. Reconnect the fiber cable.
10. Bleed the node from the pressure relief valve again to remove any remaining air.
11. Disconnect the compensation cart and reconnect the compensation circuit QD.
12. Inspect the compensator levels when finished. Fill as needed.

Draining

1. Disconnect any SeaNet cables connected to the node.
a. Install caps over the exposed penetrators.
b. Install plugs into the exposed SeaNet cables.
2. Disconnect the compensation QD closest to the node being drained.
3. Attach the compensation cart to the node QD (A).
4. Open the relief valve (B) to prevent a vacuum from forming.
5. Drain the compensation fluid from the node by turning the cart selector lever to the DRAIN position.
6. Once fluid stops flowing from the node, turn the cart selector lever to the OFF position.
